    Academic References & Sources - Knowledge Base¶

    Purpose¶

    This section catalogs the academic, industry, and regulatory sources that inform the Intelligent Investor system. All trading logic should be traceable to credible sources.

    1. Foundational Texts¶

    1.1 Value Investing & Fundamental Analysis¶

    Author Title Year Focus
    Graham, B. & Dodd, D. Security Analysis 1934/2008 Fundamental value investing
    Graham, B. The Intelligent Investor 1949/2006 Defensive/enterprising investing
    Greenblatt, J. The Little Book That Beats the Market 2006 Magic formula (ROIC + earnings yield)
    Penman, S. Financial Statement Analysis 2012 Accounting-based valuation

    Key Concepts Derived: - Margin of safety - Intrinsic value calculation - Financial statement analysis - Quality metrics (ROE, ROIC)

    1.2 Market Microstructure¶

    Author Title Year Focus
    Harris, L. Trading and Exchanges 2003 Market mechanics comprehensive
    O'Hara, M. Market Microstructure Theory 1995 Theoretical framework
    Hasbrouck, J. Empirical Market Microstructure 2007 Quantitative analysis
    Madhavan, A. Market Microstructure: A Survey 2000 Literature survey

    Key Concepts Derived: - Bid-ask spread dynamics - Order book mechanics - Price discovery - Trade execution quality

    1.3 Technical Analysis¶

    Author Title Year Focus
    Aronson, D.R. Evidence-Based Technical Analysis 2006 Statistical validation of TA
    Kirkpatrick, C. & Dahlquist, J. Technical Analysis 2015 Comprehensive TA reference
    Murphy, J.J. Technical Analysis of Financial Markets 1999 Classic TA textbook
    Pring, M.J. Technical Analysis Explained 2014 Pattern and indicator guide

    Key Concepts Derived: - Moving average systems - Momentum indicators - Support/resistance - Statistical validity of patterns

    1.4 Options & Volatility¶

    Author Title Year Focus
    Hull, J.C. Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives 2017 Definitive derivatives text
    Natenberg, S. Option Volatility and Pricing 2014 Practical volatility trading
    Sinclair, E. Volatility Trading 2013 Systematic volatility strategies
    Taleb, N.N. Dynamic Hedging 1997 Advanced risk management

    Key Concepts Derived: - Greeks (delta, gamma, theta, vega) - Implied vs realized volatility - Options pricing models - Volatility trading strategies

    Author Title Year Focus

    Author Title Year Focus

    Tharp, V.K. Trade Your Way to Financial Freedom 2006 Expectancy and sizing

    7. Reference Templates¶

    7.1 Citing Academic Sources¶

    APA Format:
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Journal,
volume(issue), pagepage. https://doi.org/xxxxx

Example:
Fama, E. F., & French, K. R. (1993). Common risk factors in the returns on
stocks and bonds. Journal of Financial Economics, 33(1), 3-56.

    7.2 Citing Regulatory Sources¶

    Format:
Agency. (Year). Title of document (Document Number if applicable).
Retrieved from URL

Example:
Securities and Exchange Commission. (2023). Market structure rules
(Release No. 34-12345). Retrieved from https://sec.gov/rules/...

    8. Research Workflow¶

    8.1 Validating a Trading Idea¶

    RESEARCH_CHECKLIST = [
    "1. Academic literature review",
    "   - Search Google Scholar, SSRN, NBER",
    "   - Find peer-reviewed papers on concept",
    "   - Note statistical significance and effect sizes",

    "2. Industry validation",
    "   - Check if practitioners use the concept",
    "   - Look for white papers from quant firms",
    "   - Review CFA Institute research",

    "3. Data requirements",
    "   - What data is needed?",
    "   - Is it point-in-time available?",
    "   - What is the data cost?",

    "4. Implementation feasibility",
    "   - Can it be coded as rules?",
    "   - What are the edge cases?",
    "   - How often does it trigger?",

    "5. Transaction cost analysis",
    "   - What is the expected turnover?",
    "   - What are spread/slippage costs?",
    "   - Is alpha > costs?",

    "6. Risk assessment",
    "   - What are the risks?",
    "   - When does it fail?",
    "   - What is the drawdown profile?"
]

    10. Bibliography Management¶

    10.1 Reference Format¶

    # Example reference entry
reference:
  id: fama_french_1993
  type: journal_article
  authors:
    - Fama, Eugene F.
    - French, Kenneth R.
  title: "Common Risk Factors in the Returns on Stocks and Bonds"
  journal: Journal of Financial Economics
  year: 1993
  volume: 33
  issue: 1
  pages: 3-56
  doi: 10.1016/0304-405X(93)90023-5
  concepts:
    - three_factor_model
    - size_effect
    - value_effect
  used_in:
    - fundamental_filters
    - factor_exposure

    Key Takeaways¶

    1. Peer-reviewed first: Validate ideas with academic research
    2. Credible sources only: Use tiered reliability scoring
    3. Official data preferred: SEC, Fed, exchanges are most reliable
    4. Cost matters: Factor in data and transaction costs
    5. Point-in-time: Avoid lookahead bias with proper data
    6. Stay current: Markets evolve, research is ongoing
    7. Document everything: Trace all logic to sources
