# Vendor Evaluation

Systematic framework for assessing vendors, suppliers, and service providers across capability, reliability, and strategic fit dimensions.

## Investigation Areas

### 1. Capability Assessment

**Core Competencies:**
- Primary services or products offered
- Specialized expertise or domain knowledge
- Technology stack and platforms
- Certifications and accreditations
- Case studies and proven track record

**Scope Alignment:**
- Does vendor capability match requirements?
- Can they deliver at required scale?
- Geographic coverage (if relevant)
- Industry-specific experience
- Customization capabilities

**Team Evaluation:**
- Team size and structure
- Key personnel qualifications
- Staff turnover rate
- Training and development programs
- Bench strength (backup resources)

**Key Questions:**
- What are the vendor's core strengths?
- Have they delivered similar projects successfully?
- Do they have domain expertise relevant to our needs?
- What's their capacity to handle our volume?

### 2. Financial Stability

**Financial Health:**
- Years in business
- Revenue trend (growing, stable, declining)
- Profitability or burn rate
- Funding status (if private)
- Credit rating (if available)

**Business Viability:**
- Customer base size and diversity
- Revenue concentration risk
- Client retention rate
- Market position and competitive standing
- Strategic direction and roadmap

**Risk Indicators:**
- Recent layoffs or restructuring
- Leadership turnover
- Office closures or downsizing
- Late payments to suppliers
- Legal or regulatory issues

**Key Questions:**
- Will this vendor be in business 3-5 years from now?
- Are they financially stable?
- Is there concentration risk (too dependent on few clients)?
- What's their growth trajectory?

### 3. Service Delivery Model

**Engagement Model:**
- Dedicated team vs. shared resources
- Onshore vs. offshore vs. hybrid
- Full-time vs. contract resources
- Staff augmentation vs. managed service
- Transition and onboarding process

**Methodology:**
- Project management approach (Agile, Waterfall, hybrid)
- Quality assurance processes
- Change management procedures
- Issue escalation and resolution
- Reporting and communication cadence

**Tools & Infrastructure:**
- Collaboration platforms
- Project tracking systems
- Knowledge management
- Development/production environments
- Security and access controls

**Key Questions:**
- What's the typical engagement structure?
- How are resources allocated and managed?
- What's the communication and reporting cadence?
- How are issues escalated and resolved?

### 4. Performance Track Record

**Reference Checks:**
- Current client references (3-5 minimum)
- Former client references (if available)
- Projects similar in scope and complexity
- Duration of relationships
- Renewal and expansion patterns

**Success Metrics:**
- On-time delivery rate
- Budget adherence
- Quality scores or defect rates
- Customer satisfaction scores (CSAT, NPS)
- Awards or recognition

**Failure Analysis:**
- Known project failures or issues
- Root causes and lessons learned
- How were problems addressed?
- What process improvements resulted?
- Client retention after issues

**Reference Check Questions:**
- How long have you worked with this vendor?
- What was the scope of work?
- How would you rate their performance? (1-10)
- Did they deliver on time and on budget?
- How did they handle issues or changes?
- Would you use them again?
- What should we watch out for?

**Key Questions:**
- What's their track record on similar projects?
- How do they perform under pressure?
- What do clients say about them?
- Have they had major failures? How handled?

### 5. Contract & Commercial Terms

**Pricing Structure:**
- Fixed price vs. time & materials
- Pricing tiers or volume discounts
- Payment terms and schedule
- Price escalation clauses
- Cost transparency

**Contract Terms:**
- Term length and renewal provisions
- Termination clauses (for cause, for convenience)
- Service level agreements (SLAs)
- Warranties and guarantees
- Liability caps and indemnification

**Intellectual Property:**
- IP ownership (client-owned vs. vendor-owned)
- License terms for vendor IP
- Work-for-hire provisions
- Pre-existing IP rights
- Source code escrow arrangements

**Performance Guarantees:**
- SLA metrics and targets
- Penalties for non-performance
- Service credits or rebates
- Escalation rights
- Audit rights

**Key Questions:**
- Are the commercial terms competitive?
- Are SLAs appropriate and enforceable?
- Who owns the IP created?
- What recourse exists for non-performance?
- Are termination provisions reasonable?

### 6. Security & Compliance

**Security Posture:**
- Security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Data encryption (at rest, in transit)
- Access controls and authentication
- Security incident response plan
- Penetration testing and vulnerability management

**Compliance:**
- Industry-specific compliance (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP)
- Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA)
- Export controls (ITAR, EAR)
- Audit frequency and results
- Insurance coverage (cyber, E&O)

**Data Handling:**
- Data location and residency
- Data retention and deletion policies
- Backup and disaster recovery
- Subprocessor list and management
- Data breach notification procedures

**Personnel Security:**
- Background check requirements
- Security training programs
- Access review procedures
- Separation of duties
- Confidentiality agreements

**Key Questions:**
- What security certifications do they hold?
- How is our data protected?
- Have they had any security breaches?
- Are they compliant with relevant regulations?
- What happens to our data upon termination?

### 7. Technology & Innovation

**Technology Stack:**
- Current technology platforms
- Cloud vs. on-premise infrastructure
- Integration capabilities (APIs, webhooks)
- Technology roadmap
- R&D investment level

**Innovation Track Record:**
- New product/service launches
- Technology patents or IP
- Industry thought leadership
- Beta programs and early adopter opportunities
- Technology partnerships

**Technical Debt:**
- Legacy system dependencies
- Migration or modernization plans
- Supported vs. end-of-life technologies
- Technical talent recruitment and retention

**Key Questions:**
- Is their technology modern and maintainable?
- Are they investing in innovation?
- Can they integrate with our systems?
- What's on their technology roadmap?
- How do they handle technology obsolescence?

### 8. Cultural & Strategic Fit

**Cultural Alignment:**
- Work style and communication preferences
- Decision-making approach
- Risk tolerance
- Values and mission alignment
- Customer service philosophy

**Strategic Alignment:**
- Long-term vision and direction
- Investment priorities
- Target market evolution
- Partnership approach
- Commitment level to client success

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- Account management structure
- Executive sponsorship
- Escalation paths
- Business review cadence
- Joint planning processes

**Key Questions:**
- Do we work well together?
- Are they responsive and collaborative?
- Do they understand our business?
- Are they invested in our success?
- Is this a transactional or partnership relationship?

| Service Delivery Model | 10% | 3 | 0.30 | Offshore concerns |
| Pricing & Commercial Terms | 15% | 4 | 0.60 | Competitive pricing |

**Weighting Guidelines:**
- Adjust weights based on what's most critical for your use case
- Mission-critical services: emphasize reliability and track record
- Innovative projects: emphasize technology and innovation
- Regulated industries: emphasize security and compliance
- Long-term partnerships: emphasize cultural fit and financial stability

## Risk Assessment

**Critical Risks:**
- Vendor financial distress or bankruptcy potential
- Security breach history or weak controls
- Key person dependency (single expert)
- Inability to meet compliance requirements
- No disaster recovery or business continuity plan

**High Risks:**
- Limited experience with required technology
- High staff turnover or recruitment challenges
- Geographic risks (political instability, natural disasters)
- Single customer concentration (>30% revenue)
- Unfavorable contract terms (lock-in, IP issues)

**Medium Risks:**
- Offshore delivery model with communication challenges
- Scaling limitations for growth
- Technology modernization needs
- Cultural misalignment
- Complex subcontractor relationships

## Data Collection Checklist

**Vendor-Provided:**
- Company overview and history
- Financial statements (if available)
- Org chart and key personnel bios
- Client list and case studies
- Security and compliance certifications
- Sample contracts and MSAs
- Insurance certificates
- Disaster recovery plan

**Independent Research:**
- Online reviews (G2, Glassdoor, Clutch)
- News and press releases
- Court records (lawsuits, judgments)
- LinkedIn analysis (size, growth, turnover)
- Competitor analysis
- Industry analyst reports

**Reference Checks:**
- Current clients (minimum 3-5)
- Former clients (if possible)
- Industry peers and partners
- Technology partners
- Analysts or consultants

**Interviews:**
- Account executive or sales lead
- Delivery manager or practice lead
- Technical architect or lead engineer
- Security/compliance officer
- Executive sponsor

## Decision Framework

**Go/No-Go Criteria:**

**Must-Have (Threshold Requirements):**
- Meets minimum capability requirements
- Financially viable (no bankruptcy risk)
- Required certifications (security, compliance)
- Acceptable reference checks (no red flags)
- Commercially reasonable terms

**Differentiators (Evaluation Factors):**
- Superior technical capability
- Proven track record in similar projects
- Cost competitiveness
- Cultural fit and responsiveness
- Innovation and thought leadership

**Selection Approach:**
1. **Screen** - Eliminate vendors not meeting must-haves
2. **Score** - Rate remaining vendors on weighted criteria
3. **Deep Dive** - Conduct detailed assessment of top 2-3
4. **Negotiate** - Finalize terms with preferred vendor
5. **Decide** - Make final selection with clear justification
