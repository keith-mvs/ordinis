# Compliance Review

Framework for assessing regulatory compliance, audit readiness, and governance structures.

## Investigation Areas

### 1. Regulatory Landscape

**Applicable Regulations:**
- Industry-specific (healthcare: HIPAA, financial services: SOX/Dodd-Frank, etc.)
- Data protection (GDPR, CCPA, PIPEDA)
- Industry standards (PCI-DSS, ISO, NIST)
- Employment law (FLSA, ADA, EEOC)
- Environmental regulations (EPA, state regulations)
- Export controls (ITAR, EAR)

**Licensing & Permits:**
- Business licenses (state, local)
- Professional licenses (if applicable)
- Industry-specific authorizations
- Operating permits
- Renewal schedules and compliance

**Jurisdiction Mapping:**
- Geographic operations and applicable laws
- Multi-state compliance requirements
- International operations and regulations
- Conflicts between jurisdictions
- Changes in regulatory landscape

**Key Questions:**
- What regulations apply to the business?
- Are there pending regulatory changes?
- What licenses/permits are required?
- Are there jurisdictional complexities?

### 2. Data Privacy & Protection

**Data Inventory:**
- Personal data types collected
- Sensitive data (PII, PHI, financial)
- Data collection methods
- Data retention periods
- Third-party data sharing

**GDPR Compliance (if applicable):**
- Legal basis for processing
- Consent mechanisms
- Data subject rights (access, erasure, portability)
- Privacy notices and transparency
- Data protection impact assessments (DPIAs)
- Data processing agreements with processors
- EU representative (if required)

**CCPA Compliance (if applicable):**
- Consumer privacy rights
- "Do Not Sell" mechanisms
- Privacy policy disclosures
- Opt-out procedures
- Service provider agreements

**Data Security:**
- Encryption standards (at rest, in transit)
- Access controls and authentication
- Data backup and recovery
- Breach notification procedures
- Incident response plan

**Key Questions:**
- What personal data is collected and why?
- Are privacy policies current and accurate?
- How are data subject rights handled?
- Has there been a data breach?
- Are data processing agreements in place?

### 3. Information Security

**Security Framework:**
- Adopted framework (ISO 27001, NIST CSF, CIS Controls)
- Security policies and procedures
- Risk assessment methodology
- Security training programs
- Incident response plan

**Access Controls:**
- Identity and access management (IAM)
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Privileged access management (PAM)
- Access review procedures
- Separation of duties

**Vulnerability Management:**
- Patch management process
- Vulnerability scanning frequency
- Penetration testing schedule
- Bug bounty program
- Security assessment results

**Security Certifications:**
- SOC 2 (Type I or Type II)
- ISO 27001
- PCI-DSS (if applicable)
- FedRAMP (if applicable)
- Audit findings and remediation

**Key Questions:**
- What security certifications are held?
- When was the last security audit?
- Have there been security incidents?
- How are vulnerabilities managed?
- Is MFA enforced for all users?

### 4. Financial & Accounting Compliance

**Financial Reporting:**
- GAAP compliance (US GAAP, IFRS)
- Revenue recognition (ASC 606)
- Lease accounting (ASC 842)
- Financial statement accuracy
- Disclosure requirements

**Tax Compliance:**
- Federal income tax compliance
- State and local tax obligations
- Sales tax/VAT collection and remittance
- Payroll tax compliance
- Transfer pricing (if international)
- Tax audit history

**SOX Compliance (if applicable):**
- Internal control over financial reporting (ICFR)
- Management assessment of controls
- External auditor attestation
- Material weaknesses or deficiencies
- Remediation plans

**Key Questions:**
- Are financial statements audited?
- Is tax compliance current?
- Are there any IRS or state tax issues?
- Are internal controls documented and tested?

### 5. Employment & Labor Law

**Employment Practices:**
- Classification (employee vs. contractor)
- Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) compliance
- Overtime calculation and payment
- Minimum wage compliance
- Record keeping requirements

**Equal Employment Opportunity:**
- Anti-discrimination policies
- Harassment prevention training
- Accommodation procedures (ADA)
- Diversity and inclusion programs
- EEO-1 reporting (if required)

**Workplace Safety:**
- OSHA compliance
- Injury/illness record keeping
- Safety training programs
- Workplace hazard assessments
- Workers' compensation insurance

**Employee Benefits:**
- ERISA compliance (if applicable)
- Health insurance (ACA compliance)
- Retirement plans (401k, pension)
- Paid leave policies (FMLA, state laws)
- COBRA administration

**Key Questions:**
- Are workers properly classified?
- Are there any employment disputes or lawsuits?
- Is workplace safety program in place?
- Are employee benefits compliant?
- Are harassment prevention measures adequate?

### 6. Intellectual Property

**IP Portfolio:**
- Patents (granted, pending)
- Trademarks (registered, common law)
- Copyrights
- Trade secrets
- Domain names

**IP Protection:**
- Confidentiality agreements (NDAs)
- IP assignment agreements (employees, contractors)
- Trade secret protection measures
- IP registration strategy
- Monitoring for infringement

**Third-Party IP:**
- Licensed technology
- Open source software usage
- License compliance
- Royalty obligations
- Restrictions on use

**IP Risks:**
- Infringement claims or litigation
- Freedom to operate analysis
- IP owned by founders vs. company
- Prior employer IP claims
- Open source license violations

**Key Questions:**
- What IP does the company own?
- Are IP assignments from founders/employees in place?
- Are there any IP disputes?
- Is open source usage compliant?
- What IP is licensed from third parties?

### 7. Contractual Compliance

**Customer Contracts:**
- Standard terms and conditions
- Service level agreements (SLAs)
- Warranty and liability provisions
- Data protection clauses
- Termination and renewal terms

**Vendor Contracts:**
- Material vendor agreements
- Service provider agreements
- Data processing agreements
- Software licenses
- Payment and renewal terms

**Contract Compliance:**
- SLA performance tracking
- Contractual obligation monitoring
- Breach notifications
- Dispute resolution procedures
- Contract renewal management

**Key Questions:**
- Are contracts properly executed?
- Are SLAs being met?
- Are there any contract breaches?
- Are contractual obligations tracked?

### 8. Corporate Governance

**Corporate Structure:**
- Entity formation and good standing
- Board composition and charter
- Committee structure (audit, compensation, etc.)
- Bylaws and operating agreements
- Shareholder/member agreements

**Board Governance:**
- Board meeting frequency and minutes
- Director independence (if applicable)
- Conflict of interest policies
- Board evaluation process
- D&O insurance

**Policies & Procedures:**
- Code of conduct
- Whistleblower policy
- Conflict of interest policy
- Document retention policy
- Anti-corruption/FCPA policy

**Fiduciary Duties:**
- Duty of care
- Duty of loyalty
- Duty of good faith
- Related party transaction approval
- Executive compensation oversight

**Key Questions:**
- Is the company in good standing?
- Are board meetings properly documented?
- Are fiduciary duties being met?
- Are governance policies in place and followed?

## Compliance Risk Matrix

**Risk Assessment:**

| Compliance Area | Regulation | Compliance Status | Risk Level | Findings | Remediation |
|-----------------|------------|-------------------|------------|----------|-------------|
| Data Privacy | GDPR | Partial | High | Missing DPIAs | 90 days |
| Security | SOC 2 | Compliant | Low | Clean audit | Ongoing |
| Tax | Federal/State | Compliant | Low | Current filings | Ongoing |
| Employment | FLSA | Non-compliant | Medium | Misclassification | 60 days |
| IP | N/A | At Risk | High | Founder IP unclear | 30 days |

**Risk Levels:**
- **Critical** - Immediate regulatory action, business disruption, or legal liability
- **High** - Significant regulatory or legal exposure, enforcement likely
- **Medium** - Compliance gaps with potential enforcement
- **Low** - Minor issues with manageable exposure

## Audit Readiness

**Audit Preparation:**
- Document retention and organization
- Policy and procedure documentation
- Evidence collection (logs, reports, attestations)
- Control testing and validation
- Gap remediation
- Mock audits or pre-assessments

**Common Audit Findings:**
- Missing or incomplete documentation
- Undocumented changes to controls
- Access rights not reviewed periodically
- Lack of segregation of duties
- Inadequate training evidence
- Policy exceptions not properly approved

**Remediation Planning:**
- Prioritize findings by risk level
- Assign ownership and timelines
- Track remediation progress
- Validate corrective actions
- Management sign-off on completion

## Red Flags

**Critical:**
- Active regulatory investigation or enforcement action
- Material non-compliance with key regulations
- Data breach with regulatory implications
- Unlicensed operations in regulated industry
- Willful non-compliance or cover-up

**High Priority:**
- Expired certifications or licenses
- Failed audit with material findings
- Outstanding regulatory violations
- Inadequate data protection measures
- Employment law violations with pending lawsuits
- IP infringement claims

**Medium Priority:**
- Outdated policies and procedures
- Incomplete compliance documentation
- Insufficient training programs
- Weak internal controls
- Third-party compliance gaps

## Compliance Program Elements

**Program Structure:**
- Compliance officer or function
- Compliance committee
- Reporting structure
- Budget and resources
- Technology systems

**Program Components:**
1. **Risk Assessment** - Identify applicable regulations and compliance risks
2. **Policies & Procedures** - Document compliance requirements and controls
3. **Training & Communication** - Educate employees on requirements
4. **Monitoring & Testing** - Verify compliance through audits and testing
5. **Reporting & Escalation** - Establish reporting channels and escalation paths
6. **Enforcement & Discipline** - Define consequences for non-compliance
7. **Continuous Improvement** - Update program based on findings and changes

**Maturity Model:**

| Level | Description | Characteristics |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 - Initial | Ad hoc, reactive | No formal program, compliance issues handled case-by-case |
| 2 - Developing | Basic program | Policies exist but inconsistently applied, limited monitoring |
| 3 - Defined | Documented | Comprehensive policies, regular training, some monitoring |
| 4 - Managed | Proactive | Well-integrated program, metrics tracked, continuous monitoring |
| 5 - Optimized | Strategic | Culture of compliance, predictive analytics, continuous improvement |
