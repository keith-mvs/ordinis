# Market Analysis

Framework for evaluating market opportunities, competitive dynamics, and positioning strategies.

## Investigation Areas

### 1. Market Size & Growth

**Total Addressable Market (TAM):**
- Top-down calculation (market size × adoption rate)
- Bottom-up calculation (price × potential customers)
- Market research firm estimates (Gartner, IDC, Forrester)
- Multiple methodologies for validation

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
- Geographic constraints
- Segment focus (SMB, mid-market, enterprise)
- Product/solution fit limitations
- Regulatory restrictions

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM):**
- Realistic market share estimate (Year 1, Year 3, Year 5)
- Go-to-market constraints
- Competitive positioning
- Resource limitations

**Growth Dynamics:**
- Historical growth rate (CAGR last 3-5 years)
- Projected growth rate (next 3-5 years)
- Growth drivers (technology, regulation, demographic shifts)
- Headwinds and constraints
- Inflection points or catalysts

**Key Questions:**
- How is market size calculated? (Be specific about methodology)
- What assumptions underlie the projections?
- Are we in early adopter phase, growth phase, or maturity?
- What could accelerate or decelerate growth?

### 2. Competitive Landscape

**Competitor Identification:**
- Direct competitors (same solution, same customer)
- Indirect competitors (different solution, same problem)
- Adjacent players (could expand into space)
- Potential new entrants (high-resource players)

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Market share distribution (leader, challengers, niche players)
- Differentiation dimensions (price, features, service, brand)
- Competitive moats (network effects, switching costs, scale advantages)
- Strengths and weaknesses by competitor

**Competitive Analysis Framework:**

| Competitor | Market Share | Strengths | Weaknesses | Strategy | Threat Level |
|------------|--------------|-----------|------------|----------|--------------|
| Player A   | 35%          | ...       | ...        | ...      | High         |
| Player B   | 20%          | ...       | ...        | ...      | Medium       |

**Market Structure:**
- Is this a winner-take-all market? (Network effects, high switching costs)
- Or is there room for multiple successful players?
- What's the fragmentation level?
- Are there emerging consolidation trends?

**Key Questions:**
- Who are the top 3-5 competitors?
- What do customers say about competitive alternatives?
- Where are competitors investing?
- What would it take to displace the incumbent?

### 3. Customer Segmentation

**Target Personas:**
- Demographic characteristics (industry, company size, geography)
- Psychographic characteristics (values, attitudes, behaviors)
- Jobs to be done (what problem are they solving?)
- Buying behavior (decision process, budget cycle, procurement)

**Segment Analysis:**

| Segment | Size | Growth | Willingness to Pay | Acquisition Cost | Attractiveness |
|---------|------|--------|-------------------|------------------|----------------|
| SMB     | 1M   | 15%    | $50/mo            | $200             | Medium         |
| MM      | 100K | 10%    | $500/mo           | $2K              | High           |

**Customer Needs Hierarchy:**
- Must-have requirements (table stakes)
- Performance requirements (differentiators)
- Delight factors (unexpected value adds)

**Unmet Needs:**
- What are customers struggling with?
- What workarounds are they using?
- What would they pay more for?
- What adjacent problems could be solved?

**Key Questions:**
- Who is the ideal customer profile (ICP)?
- What segments are underserved?
- How do needs vary by segment?
- Where is customer concentration risk?

### 4. Market Trends & Dynamics

**Technology Trends:**
- Enabling technologies (AI/ML, cloud, mobile, IoT)
- Platform shifts (SaaS adoption, mobile-first, API economy)
- Emerging capabilities creating new opportunities
- Technology obsolescence risks

**Regulatory Environment:**
- Current regulations affecting market
- Pending regulatory changes
- Compliance requirements by geography
- Regulatory risk or opportunity

**Economic Factors:**
- Economic sensitivity (recession-resistant vs. discretionary)
- Budget availability in target segments
- Pricing pressure trends
- Macroeconomic indicators

**Social/Demographic Shifts:**
- Workforce changes (remote work, gig economy)
- Generational preferences
- Urbanization or geographic shifts
- Cultural trends affecting adoption

**Key Questions:**
- What macro trends favor this market?
- What trends pose threats?
- How sustainable are current market dynamics?
- What black swan events could reshape the market?

### 5. Customer Acquisition & Economics

**Go-to-Market Channels:**
- Direct sales (field sales, inside sales)
- Channel partners (resellers, VARs, ISVs)
- Self-service/product-led growth
- Marketplace listings (AWS, Salesforce AppExchange)

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**
- CAC by channel
- CAC by customer segment
- CAC trends over time
- Payback period (months to recover CAC)

**Customer Lifetime Value (LTV):**
- Average revenue per account (ARPA)
- Gross margin
- Retention/churn rate
- Expansion revenue potential
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: 3:1 or higher)

**Sales Efficiency:**
- Sales cycle length
- Win rate
- Average deal size
- Quota attainment
- Sales productivity metrics

**Key Questions:**
- What's the most efficient acquisition channel?
- How does CAC scale with growth?
- What's the bottleneck in sales process?
- How does unit economics vary by segment?

### 6. Barriers to Entry & Exit

**Entry Barriers:**
- Capital requirements
- Technical complexity
- Regulatory approval process
- Network effects or switching costs
- Brand recognition requirements
- Access to distribution channels

**Exit Barriers:**
- Customer contracts and commitments
- Switching costs (data migration, retraining)
- Integration dependencies
- Regulatory requirements
- Brand/reputation damage

**Competitive Moat Assessment:**
- How defendable is the position?
- What prevents new entrants?
- What prevents customers from leaving?
- How long does the advantage last?

**Key Questions:**
- What would it cost a new entrant to compete?
- Could a big tech company easily enter this space?
- What protects against commoditization?
- How strong are customer lock-in effects?

### 7. Strategic Positioning

**Value Proposition:**
- Unique value delivered to customers
- Differentiation from alternatives
- Quantified value (ROI, time saved, cost reduction)
- Emotional benefits

**Positioning Statement:**
- For [target customer]
- Who [customer need]
- Our [product/service]
- That [key benefit]
- Unlike [competitive alternative]
- We [unique differentiation]

**Brand Perception:**
- Brand awareness (aided vs. unaided)
- Brand attributes (innovative, reliable, premium, affordable)
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)
- Customer satisfaction scores

**Key Questions:**
- What's the 30-second elevator pitch?
- How do customers describe the value?
- What's the perception vs. reality gap?
- Is positioning consistent across channels?

## Risk Flags

**Critical:**
- Market declining or contracting
- Dominant competitor with 70%+ market share and strong moat
- Commoditization with no differentiation
- Regulatory existential threat
- Technology disruption making solution obsolete

**High Priority:**
- Highly fragmented market with low barriers to entry
- Customer concentration risk (>20% revenue from single customer)
- Long sales cycles (>12 months) with low close rates
- Negative unit economics (LTV < CAC)
- Unfavorable market timing (too early or too late)

**Medium Priority:**
- Mature market with low growth
- Strong competitors with deep pockets
- Complex buying process with many stakeholders
- Seasonal or cyclical demand patterns
- Geographic concentration risk

## Analysis Framework

**Porter's Five Forces:**
1. **Competitive Rivalry** - How intense is current competition?
2. **Threat of New Entrants** - How easy is it for new players to enter?
3. **Threat of Substitutes** - What alternative solutions exist?
4. **Buyer Power** - How much leverage do customers have?
5. **Supplier Power** - How much leverage do suppliers have?

**SWOT Analysis:**
- **Strengths** - Internal advantages and capabilities
- **Weaknesses** - Internal limitations and gaps
- **Opportunities** - External favorable conditions
- **Threats** - External challenges and risks
